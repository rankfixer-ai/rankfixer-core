## Signal-by-Signal Analysis

### 1. Schema Completeness (25% weight, Avg: 59.3%)

**The most fixable signal.** Schema.org markup is the #1 differentiator between AI-visible and AI-invisible domains.

- 68% of domains lack `Organization` schema on their homepage
- 82% lack `FAQPage` schema on key landing pages
- 74% omit `sameAs` links that connect the brand to authoritative sources
- Only 4% implement the full recommended schema suite

**Quickest win:** Add JSON-LD `Organization` + `WebSite` schema to homepage. 1-hour fix. 10-15 point gain.

### 2. Entity Density (20% weight, Avg: 44.2%)

LLMs extract entities (people, organizations, products, places) to build a knowledge graph of your site.

- Average: 3.2 entity mentions per 1000 words (target: 8+)
- Most domains mention their brand as plain text, not as a schema-tagged entity
- Product and feature names are rarely marked up as entities
- Entity co-occurrence (linking related entities with @id) is almost non-existent outside the top 10

**Quickest win:** Tag brand name as `schema.org Organization` entity with `@id`. Link all mentions to this entity. 2-hour fix.

### 3. Content Answer Density (20% weight, Avg: 51.7%)

AI models prefer direct, declarative answers over marketing prose.

- Marketing-heavy landing pages dominate; few direct answer formats
- FAQ content hidden in JavaScript accordions — invisible to AI crawlers
- H2/H3 tags used for visual styling, not semantic structure
- Average "fluff ratio" (non-declarative sentences): 41%

**Quickest win:** Add an FAQ section on your top 5 pages using semantic HTML + FAQPage schema. 3-hour fix.

### 4. Technical Signals (15% weight, Avg: 62.4%)

The foundation layer. Good scores here, but common AI-specific issues.

- 39% block GPTBot in robots.txt
- 41% block ClaudeBot in robots.txt
- 96% have no llms.txt file
- 47% fail Core Web Vitals (LCP > 2.5s on mobile)

**Quickest win:** Unblock AI crawlers in robots.txt. Add an llms.txt file. 5-minute fix. 10-15 point gain.

### 5. Backlink Quality (10% weight, Avg: 38.1%)

LLMs use backlinks as authority signals — similar to, but distinct from, Google's PageRank.

- Top performers average DA 80+; bottom performers average DA 30-
- .edu and .gov backlinks are rare but heavily weighted
- AI models appear to weigh backlinks from AI-indexed knowledge bases more heavily

### 6. Brand Entity Recognition (5% weight, Avg: 33.6%)

Is your brand a "known entity" in the knowledge bases LLMs are trained on?

- 91% of domains have no Wikipedia page
- 76% have no Crunchbase profile
- 74% omit `sameAs` links in schema markup
- Brands with Wikipedia + Crunchbase + schema `sameAs` score 3x higher

### 7. Freshness (5% weight, Avg: 52.9%)

AI models weigh recently-updated content more heavily.

- Many domains show original publish dates from 2022-2024 with no `dateModified`
- Static copyright footers ("© 2024") are a negative signal
- Blog content freshness correlates with overall score (r=0.61)

## The "Ghost" Problem: 36% Score Below 20

These domains are effectively invisible to AI. They share common traits:

- No schema.org markup detected
- AI crawlers blocked in robots.txt
- JavaScript-only rendering with no SSR fallback
- No knowledge base presence (Wikipedia, Crunchbase)
- Content structured for human readers only (no semantic HTML)
- Missing or outdated Core Web Vitals

**The tragedy:** Most of these domains rank well on Google. They have good products. But AI models have no idea they exist.

## Recommendations

### For All Domains (Universal Fixes)

1. **Add Organization + WebSite schema** (1 hour, +10-15 points)
2. **Create an llms.txt file** (5 minutes, +3-5 points)
3. **Unblock AI crawlers in robots.txt** (5 minutes, +10-15 points)
4. **Add FAQPage schema to top 5 pages** (3 hours, +8-12 points)

### For Tier 3-5 Domains (Score < 60)

Prioritize Quick Wins (under 1 hour each) to build momentum:
1. Unblock AI crawlers
2. Add basic schema
3. Create llms.txt
4. Update content freshness signals

### For Tier 1-2 Domains (Score 60+)

Focus on strategic improvements:
1. Build knowledge base presence (Wikipedia, Crunchbase)
2. Increase entity density with @id linking
3. Publish original research/data for citation
4. Improve backlink quality from AI-indexed sources
