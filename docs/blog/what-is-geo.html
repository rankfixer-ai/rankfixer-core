What Is GEO? Generative Engine Optimization Explained

Published 2026-07-11 · 5 min read

Search is no longer the only way people find you. Increasingly, they ask an AI — ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Claude — and get an answer that quietly cites a handful of sources. If your site isn't one of them, you're invisible to a fast-growing slice of discovery.

GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) is the practice of structuring and presenting your content so large language models choose to cite it when they answer questions in your niche. Think of it as SEO for the answer box that isn't a box — it's a sentence with a footnote.

Why GEO matters now

  • Traffic is shifting. AI assistants summarize the web instead of listing ten blue links. A citation in that summary is the new rank #1.
  • Citations compound. Once an LLM learns your entity, it tends to reuse it across related prompts.
  • It's a different game than SEO. Keyword density matters less than clarity, structure, and machine-readable context.

What LLMs actually look for

Models don't "read" like humans. They extract: clear answer-first writing, explicit entity definitions, well-formed schema markup, and consistent facts. A tool like Rankfixer scores your site across the seven signals that predict whether you'll be cited.

Getting started

  1. Run a free AI visibility check on your domain.
  2. Fix the top gaps (usually schema + answer structure).
  3. Publish content that directly answers the questions your buyers ask AI.

GEO isn't about tricking models — it's about being unambiguously, usefully citable. Do that, and the citations follow.
