The 7 Signals LLMs Use to Decide What to Cite

Published 2026-07-11 · 6 min read

When an LLM answers a question, it doesn't flip a coin on sources. It leans on structural signals that make a page easy to extract, verify, and rephrase. Rankfixer scores your site on seven of them.

1. Schema completeness

Structured data (JSON-LD) tells a model exactly what your page is — an Article, a Product, an FAQ. Pages with correct schema are far more likely to be parsed as a citable fact.

2. Answer structure

Models love a question followed immediately by a direct answer. Lead with the answer; elaborate after. Bulleted and numbered lists are extracted cleanly.

3. Entity density

Clear, repeated naming of your brand, product, and topic entities helps a model build a stable "mental model" of what you are — and cite you consistently.

4. Crawlability for AI bots

If you block GPTBot, Google-Extended, or PerplexityBot in robots.txt, you've opted out of being cited. Make sure AI crawlers are allowed.

5. Factual consistency

Contradictions across your site erode trust. Models prefer sources that agree with themselves and with the broader web.

6. Authority signals

Backlinks, citations from other sites, and a clean author/Organization schema signal that you're a source worth quoting.

7. Freshness

Recently updated, dated content reads as current. Stale pages are deprioritized for time-sensitive queries.
