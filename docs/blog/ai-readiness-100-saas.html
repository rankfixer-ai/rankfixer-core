What is "AI Readiness"? (The Methodology)

Traditional SEO measures keywords and backlinks. AI Readiness — Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) — measures how easily a Large Language Model can extract, comprehend, and cite your website as a factual source.

Using the open-source RankFixer Core engine, we scored each of the 100 SaaS websites on 7 primary LLM citation signals:

SignalWeightWhat We Measured
Schema Completeness25%JSON-LD, Microdata, RDFa; FAQPage, Article, Product schemas
Entity Density20%Brand mentions, @id references, semantic clustering
Content Answer Density20%Direct answers vs. fluff; declarative sentences
Technical Signals15%Core Web Vitals, robots.txt AI crawler access
Backlink Quality10%Domain Authority, trust flow
Brand Entity Recognition5%Organization schema with sameAs links
Freshness5%dateModified in schema

3 Shocking Findings

1. The "Schema Ceiling" is Real

Most SaaS companies have basic Organization schema. But only ~11% utilize FAQPage, and almost none use HowTo or SoftwareApplication.

The Fix: Add FAQPage schema to your top 10 pages. 5 minutes. 8-12 point gain.

2. The "Wall of Text" Penalty

Sites with dense, unbroken paragraphs score significantly lower than sites with structured, scannable content. LLMs process in tokens — if your value prop is buried in a 500-word essay, the AI won't extract it.

3. The "Average" is Dangerously Low

The median score across 100 top SaaS companies was just 45.0/100. This is your opportunity — a 32-point gap between top and bottom quartiles, closable with basic optimizations.

  3. Update your copyright year
